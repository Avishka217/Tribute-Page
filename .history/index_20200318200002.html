NIKOLA TESLA

The Man who invented the Twentieth Century

Here's a time line of Nikola Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Nikola Tesla was born on the 10th of July in the former Austrian Empire(present Croatia)
  • 1881 - Moved to Budapest, Hungary to work at the Budapest Telephone Exchange as the chief electrician
  • 1884 - Moved to the USA to work at the Edison Machine Works in New York City.He worked for 6 months.
  • 1885 - Received his first US patent and started Tesla Electric Light and Manufacturing. It was an arc lighting Manufacturing and utility company. He worked for the rest of the year obtaining the patents including an improved DC generator.
  • 1886 - Tesla lost his investors and was forced out.
  • 1889 - He invented the tesla coil. Tesla came up with his Tesla coil with an air gap instead of insulating material between the primary and secondary windings and an iron core that could be moved to different positions in or out of the coil.
  • 1895 - Designed one of the first hydroelectric power plants in the United States, at Niagara Falls
  • 1900 - He became obsessed with the wireless transmission of energy and worked on a project to build a global, wireless communication system
  • 1915 - Tesla abandoned his project due to competition from Guglielmo Marconi and Thomas Edison
  • 1943 - Tesla died of coronary thrombosis on January 7 at the age of 86 in New York City, where he had lived for nearly 60 years.
